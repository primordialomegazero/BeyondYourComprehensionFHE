\begin{abstract}
For 17 years, FHE bootstrapping research focused on optimizing the decryption circuit—blind rotation, key switching, modulus reduction. We demonstrate that bootstrapping is fundamentally a convergence problem governed by two principles absent from traditional FHE: the golden ratio $\varphi = 1.618\ldots$ and Lyapunov stability theory. Together, they reveal that optimal noise convergence follows $\varphi^{-1}$-weighted decay with exponential stability guaranteed by Lyapunov exponent $\lambda = \ln(\varphi) \approx 0.4812$. We provide the complete mathematical framework, the core implementation, and experimental validation across four independent FHE libraries.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Since Gentry's 2009 construction, FHE bootstrapping has been approached as a circuit evaluation problem: homomorphically compute the decryption function to refresh ciphertext noise. This assumption produced TFHE's blind rotation, CKKS's key switching, and HElib's thin bootstrapping—each requiring thousands of operations.

We show that bootstrapping is not a circuit problem. It is a convergence problem.

\section{Mathematical Framework}

\subsection{The Optimal Decay Rate}

For iterative noise reduction to be stable and optimal, the decay rate $r$ must satisfy:
\begin{equation}
    r = 1 - r \implies r^2 + r - 1 = 0 \implies r = \frac{\sqrt{5} - 1}{2} = \varphi^{-1}
\end{equation}
This is the unique solution. Any other rate either converges too slowly or produces oscillation.

\subsection{Lyapunov Stability}

Define noise evolution:
\begin{equation}
    n_{k+1} = n_k \cdot \varphi^{-1} + 40 \cdot (1 - \varphi^{-1})
\end{equation}
Let error $e_k = n_k - 40$. Then:
\begin{equation}
    e_{k+1} = e_k \cdot \varphi^{-1} \implies |e_k| = |e_0| \cdot \varphi^{-k} = |e_0| \cdot e^{-k \ln(\varphi)}
\end{equation}
The Lyapunov exponent $\lambda = \ln(\varphi) \approx 0.4812 > 0$ proves exponential convergence to the fixed point $n^* = 40$ bits.

\begin{theorem}[$\varphi$-Harmonic Convergence]
For any $n_0 \in [0, 140]$, the process $n_{k+1} = n_k \cdot \varphi^{-1} + 40 \cdot (1 - \varphi^{-1})$ converges exponentially to $n^* = 40$ with Lyapunov exponent $\lambda = \ln(\varphi)$. No other decay rate achieves faster stable convergence.
\end{theorem}
